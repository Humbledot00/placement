Resume Structure


1. Name

Include your name


2. Contact Information

Include your name, phone number, email address, and LinkedIn profile.


3. Summary or Objective (Optional)

A brief statement summarizing your skills and career goals.


4. Education

Include your degrees, school names, and graduation dates.


5. Work Experience

List your most recent internship. Include title, company name, dates, and key responsibilities.


6. Skills

List relevant skills tailored to the job you're applying for.


7. Projects

List your projects, including title, dates, and key responsibilities.


8. Certifications & Achievements

Include any certifications or notable achievements relevant to the position.

Do’s and Don’ts


Don’ts

1. No Declaration

Avoid adding unnecessary declarations or statements at the end.

2. No Fancy Colors

Stick to professional colors. Avoid bright or overly decorative fonts and colors.

3. No "Languages Known" Section

Unless directly relevant to the job, avoid listing basic language proficiencies.

4. No Paragraphs

Avoid adding long Paragraphs.

5. Brief Internship Details

Provide only 1-2 lines for internships; highlight essential skills and contributions.

6. No Photographs/ Profile Photo

Do’s

1. Font Size and Font Style

Use a standard font size of 11 or 12 for readability and fonts like Times New Roman or Calibri for a clean look.

2. Use Bullet Points

Present information in bullet points for clarity and easy scanning.

3. Equal Spacing

Ensure consistent spacing throughout the resume for a well-organized layout.

4. Quantify Content

Include numbers and measurable achievements (e.g., "Managed a team of 5" or "Increased sales by 15%").

5. Use Reverse Chronological Order

List experiences starting with the most recent to highlight recent accomplishments.
